Introduction

Right now, the world is in the need of rapid vaccination drives which will allow for herd immunity, which is the quickest way to come out of Covid-19 pandemic and return to normalcy.

Due to personal prejudices, local politics or orthodox beliefs, we have a considerable amount of population who are against vaccination drives. With the rise of social media, the dissemination of information (hence, potentially, misinformation) has become easier than ever before. Unsurprisingly, anti-vaccine activists have also began to utilize platforms like Twitter to share their views. As a result, their activism has expanded jurisdictions to include online propaganda.

The results from this project will help CDC focus on what areas to primarily focus on, to improve vaccination awareness.

Problem Statement

This project tends to determine the anti vaccine tweets related to covid-19 by collecting tweets from the twitter API and performing various algorithms to filter out the anti vaccine tweets . Using the location of these tweets’ source, the locations were plotted on the world map.

Data and Approach

The dataset consists of Covid 19 vaccination tweets. The data collected from the twitter stream captures all languages, but the higher prevalence are English, Spanish, and French. The Structure of the data is: user’s tweet_id, date, timestamp, language of the tweet and the country code. These attributes can be used to mine the tweets, to find out the various regions where people have tweeted in favor or against the vaccines.

Based on the geographical location-based information extracted after clustering the anti vaccine related tweets, the government/ CDC can focus on educating the populace about benefits of covid 19 vaccine in those areas.

Methodologies

Preprocessing

  • The data was collected from twitter by applying for the Developers Account for academic research access.
  • The twitter IDs available for Covid tweets were hydrated to get the actual data.
  • The data was completely unlabelled, with just the tweets as data.
  • The dataset had tweets in all languages. They were filtered and only English ones were chosen.
  • Data cleaning was done in the following steps
    • URLs were removed
    • # of hashtags were removed
    • Emoticons were filtered out
    • Any letter repeating more than twice was replaced with 2 occurrences of that letter
  • Only columns “tweet” and “location” were used

K Means Clustering

  • The tweets were further filtered to include only those which had words related to “vaccine”.
  • The tweets were converted to sentence embedding Ks using an existing model.
  • Three initial data points were given,namely - anti-vaxxer, neutral and for-vaxxer.
  • Using cosine similarity between the K points and tweets with the sentence embeddings, the tweets were clustered into the three clusters (anti-vaxxer, neutral and for-vaxxer)
  • The algorithm was run to 10 epochs as the dataset was too large and took lots of time.
  • Unfortunately kmeans resulted in many false positives and did not result in any useful information.
  • So another attempt to use a different algorithm was made.

Bag of Words

  • A dictionary of anti-vaccine keywords was created with words like (NoVaccineForMe, Don'tGetVaccinated, etc)
  • The presence of any of the anti vaccine keywords in any tweets was checked.
  • If such a keyword was found, those tweets were labelled as antiVax tweets.
  • Using this label, the anti vaccine tweets were filtered.
  • This resultant categorization was further used to plot the locations on the world map.

Neural Network

  • We found a very small, labelled, vax and antivax dataset (around 1000 tweets) on Github. (https://github.com/XiaoyiYuan/vaccination_online_discussion)
  • A deep neural network was built to classify this dataset.
  • Logistic regression, which is a simple form of a neural network that classifies data categorically was used in this project.
  • The data was divided into training and testing set in a ratio of 80% to 20% respectively.
  • An accuracy of 80% was achieved on the test set.
  • This model was then used to label the data we collected.
  • The resultant categorization was further used to visualize the anti-vaxxers on the world map.

Visualization and Analysis

The data was received from the Neural networks and the Bag Of Words algorithm was used to plot the resultant locations of anti-Vaxx tweets on the world map. For this initially, from geopy.exc, GeocoderTimedOut was imported and from geopy.geocoders, Nominatim was imported. Finally after removing the null or ambiguous columns, the locations that were mentioned in the ‘location’ column were converted into their respective latitude and longitude. Using these points, the locations were plotted on the world map for the data received from both 1. Neural Networks and 2. Bag Of Words

  • Map Visualization for Neural Network

By observing this plot we see that, mostly the places with the majority of anti-vaxx tweets are in the USA, UK, Australia, and a few parts of Africa and Asia. In Usa We can see that mainly the central and eastern states have high clusters of anti-vaxx tweets. But this algorithm gave lots of false positives

  • Map visualization for Bag Of Words

Bag of words algorithm used a predefined dictionary and hence only selected the tweets that matched a specific words criteria. Hence the number of false positives was very less and gave more accurate information about the locations. We can see that a lot of central america is in the influence of anti-vaccination along with few states in the east and south. Us is also predominant with anti-vaxx tweets and few in the coastal regions of australia.

  • Word Cloud for Bag Of Words:

This word cloud has been plotted using the resultant data from the Bag Of Words algorithm. In this We can see The countries, states and cities based on their frequency of occurrence. It gives a visual impact of the data and can help us point the places on the map.

Results

  • For the K Means clustering, it was observed that the clusters were formed more on the negative sentiment about the vaccine (such as countries not getting vaccine, vaccines not available, etc) rather than being about anti-vaxxers, even though initial points were chosen as that.
  • The Neural Network performed slightly better than K Means, giving lesser false positives. But it still had the same issue, general sentiment rather than vaccine oriented.
  • TThe Bag of Words used hand picked keywords, so it performed better. It had some False Negatives, but very low False Positives.

Observations and Conclusions

  • As seen in the word cloud of the location of anti-vaxxer tweets, many major countries were found. States such as Texas, Arizona, Michigan, Georgia, NY, Louisiana are seen. The Eastern coast has more such tweets than the West Coast as seen in the map.
  • Since these states have high populations, further analysis needs to be done based on which areas exactly are these tweets originating from. Since the tweets were selected only for English Language, the locations were found mostly from those countries that have a predominance of English users. Hence many countries with non English speakers couldn't be plotted as the tweets are in various languages Also a lot of data did not have anything mentioned in the location column or had ambiguous places like ‘home’, ‘planet earth’ etc. which put limitations on the exact places. But still a considerable amount of information and clusters have been obtained using the above algorithms and performing exploratory analysis. The application of this project is that the government can then look into the matter and help with the education and awareness that surrounds this cause.
